<instructions>
A *canonical index entry* for a document consists of four fields, any of which
might be empty if the information is not available:

- Headline: A concise title for the document, ideally including the document type and the name. The headline should capture everything known about the purpose of the document.
- Date: The date of the document, in ISO format (YYYY-MM-DD).
- People: People directly involved in the creation, dissemination, or receipt of the document, or who are the subject of the document. For interview transcripts, these are the interviewer(s) and interviewee(s).
- Agency or agencies: The law enforcement or other organization(s) listed on the letterhead or affiliated with the authors or recipients of the document.

Review the extracted metadata, and create a canonical index entry for this document. Note that the metadata may include additional pieces of information beyond those needed for the index entry; only include the requested fields and information. Do not add additional fields. For the "People" field, only include document authors, senders, recipients, interviewers, interviewees, or the subjects of the doucment, even if other people are mentioned in the metadata. For the date field, only include the date of the document itself, not incident dates or other dates. For the "Agency or agencies" field, only include the agency associated with the document itself, via the letterhead/header or the affiliation of the author.

There may be various dates mentioned in the metadata. We are ONLY interested in the date of the document itself, not incident dates or other dates. If the document is an interview transcript, then the date should be the date of the interview. For a letter or memo, the date should be the date on the letterhead/header. For communications, we are interested in the date the communication was sent; however if only the receipt date is available, use that. Many documents contain one or more signatures with dates, we are only interested in the date the document itself was created/sent, not the dates of the signatures; however, if no other date is available, use the date of the earliest signature.
</instructions>
