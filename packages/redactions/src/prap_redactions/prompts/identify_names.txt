Extract names of individuals who are:
1. Victims - Individuals who have suffered harm from a criminal act. The specific criminal act must be named within the document. Criminal acts include domestic violence, sexual assault, assault, etc.
2. Witnesses - Individuals who observed or have knowledge about an incident (EXCLUDING police officers)
3. Children - Minors who are victims or witnesses in the case

Extract ANY identifiable name or reference, including:
- Full proper names (e.g., "Jonathan Brown", "Sarah J. Miller")
- Partial names with titles (e.g., "Mrs. Johnson", "Mr. Thompson")
- First names only if they clearly refer to a victim or witness (e.g., "Jane said she was assaulted")
- Last names only if they clearly refer to a victim or witness (e.g., "Smith witnessed the incident")
</objective>

<names_to_avoid>
## DO NOT EXTRACT
- Names of defendants, suspects, or perpetrators
- Names of law enforcement personnel (officers, detectives, etc.) EVEN IF they are witnesses
- Names of legal professionals (attorneys, judges, court staff)
- Names of government officials unrelated to victim/witness roles
- Descriptive identifiers that aren't names (e.g., "female subject," "unknown witness")
- Coded references (e.g., "CV," "W," "(V)")
- Relationship descriptors WITHOUT names (e.g., just "wife" or "mother" without any name)
- Age-based identifiers WITHOUT names (e.g., "elderly woman" with no name given)
- Role-based references WITHOUT names (e.g., just "hostage" or "victim" with no name)
</names_to_avoid>

<thinking_process_for_names>
1. Is there ANY name or identifiable reference to this person (full name, partial name, title + surname, etc.)? If not, DO NOT extract.
2. Is there clear evidence this person is a victim or witness? If not, DO NOT extract.
3. Is this person definitely NOT in any of the excluded categories (law enforcement, defendant, legal professional)? If they are excluded, DO NOT extract.
4. Does surrounding context confirm their role as victim or witness? If uncertain, DO NOT extract.
5. Is there ANY indication this person is a police officer or law enforcement? If yes, DO NOT extract even if they are also a witness.
</thinking_process_for_names>


<thinking_process>
Follow this systematic approach:
1. First, scan the document for clear indicators of victim/witness status:
   - Direct references: "victim," "witness," "complainant," "survivor," "minor"
   - Contextual clues: "testified that," "was assaulted," "reported that," "suffered injuries"

2. For each potential name you find (including partial names):
   - Check if there is ANY identifiable name or reference (full, partial, title + surname, etc.)
   - Check if there are ANY indicators of law enforcement role:
     * Titles like "Officer," "Detective," "Deputy," "Sergeant," "Sheriff," etc.
     * References to "badge," "precinct," "department," etc.
     * Context about taking reports, responding to calls, or other police functions
     * If ANY law enforcement indicators exist, mark for exclusion EVEN IF they are witnesses
   - Determine their likely role based on surrounding context
   - Assess confidence level in this determination
   - Collect the exact text that supports your classification

3. For ambiguous cases:
   - Look for additional mentions of the same name elsewhere in the document
   - Analyze the broader narrative to infer their role
   - If still uncertain after thorough analysis, DO NOT extract the name
   - If there's ANY possibility they are law enforcement, DO NOT extract the name

4. Be particularly attentive to privacy-sensitive individuals:
   - Children are often referenced with initials or partial names
   - Sexual assault victims may be referred to as "Jane Doe" or by initials
   - Domestic violence victims may be referenced by relationship ("defendant's wife") or partial names

5. Apply the verification steps to each potential name:
   - First test: Is there ANY identifiable name or reference?
   - Second test: Is there clear evidence of victim/witness role?
   - Third test: Is this person NOT in any excluded category (especially law enforcement)?
   - Fourth test: Does context confirm their classification?
   - Fifth test: Is there ANY indication they might be law enforcement? If yes, EXCLUDE.

   Only extract names that pass ALL verification steps.
</thinking_process>
