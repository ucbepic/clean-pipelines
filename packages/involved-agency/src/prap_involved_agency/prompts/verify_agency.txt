<common_agency_types>
Use this checklist during the missed-agency recovery step to ensure no common agency types

TYPICALLY RESPONDING:
- City Police Departments (e.g., Los Angeles Police Department, San Jose Police Department)

<verification_rules>
RULE 1 — NO FEDERAL AGENCIES:
FBI, DEA, ATF, U.S. Marshals, Border Patrol, DHS, ICE → REJECTED regardless of evidence.
NOTE: State-level agencies (California DOJ, California Highway Patrol, state corrections) are
NOT federal and should NOT be rejected under this rule.

RULE 2 — GEOGRAPHIC IDENTIFIER REQUIRED:
Agency name must start with city or county name (or be a state-level agency).
If missing, search the source document for geographic context. If found, correct it. If not found, REJECT.

RULE 3 — PARENT AGENCY ONLY:
Remove sub-divisions, bureaus, units, station names, court locations.
Normalize to parent agency name.

RULE 4 — LAW ENFORCEMENT AND OVERSIGHT AGENCIES ONLY:
Exclude fire departments, EMS, hospitals, and multi-agency task forces.
INCLUDE formal government oversight bodies with investigative authority (OIG, Police
Accountability departments, Police Commissions with investigative mandates).
</verification_rules>

<explicit_linkage_check>
This is the most important check. For each piece of evidence:

1. Find the passage in the source document
2. Confirm the AGENCY NAME and the ACTION VERB are clearly connected within the same passage
   (same statement or closely adjacent sentences where the officer-agency relationship is established)
3. If the evidence says "Officer X did Z" and the agency name appears in a nearby sentence establishing
   Officer X as a member of that agency, this IS acceptable linkage.
4. If the agency name only appears in a completely separate section (e.g., a roster, header, or
   unrelated paragraph), this is NOT acceptable linkage. Mark as REJECTED.

For INVESTIGATING: The source must contain a passage where [Agency Name] + [verb about investigating USE OF FORCE or MISCONDUCT] are clearly connected.
Valid: investigated the shooting, reviewed whether force was justified, declined charges against the officer, determined cause of death, investigated officer misconduct
Invalid: investigated the burglary, investigated a call for service, interviewed crime witnesses (these are RESPONDING actions)

For RESPONDING: The source must contain a passage where Officer [Name] of/from [Agency Name] + [incident verb] are clearly connected.
Valid: responded, used force, fired weapon, pursued, was present, secured scene, violated policy, investigated the underlying crime
</explicit_linkage_check>

<critical_investigating_vs_responding_distinction>
INVESTIGATING = investigated WHETHER THE OFFICER'S FORCE WAS JUSTIFIED or WHETHER THE OFFICER COMMITTED MISCONDUCT
RESPONDING = officers were involved in the incident, INCLUDING investigating the underlying crime

The agency that investigated the burglary/robbery/assault is RESPONDING.
The agency that investigated whether the officers shooting was lawful is INVESTIGATING.

If the proposed type is INVESTIGATING but the evidence shows the agency investigated the underlying crime (not the force), correct the type to RESPONDING.
If the proposed type is INVESTIGATING but the evidence only shows passive actions like was notified or received report, REJECT entirely.
</critical_investigating_vs_responding_distinction>

<verification_steps>
Step 1: Apply Rules 1-4. If any rule causes rejection, stop and return REJECTED.

Step 2: Locate each evidence passage in the source document.
- Is the evidence actually present in the source text?
- Does the passage clearly connect the agency name to the action verb?
- If evidence cannot be found or lacks clear linkage, mark as REJECTED.

Step 3: Independently determine agency type from the evidence.
- Verbs about investigating use of force or misconduct → INVESTIGATING
- Incident involvement verbs OR investigating the underlying crime → RESPONDING
- Both use-of-force-investigation AND incident involvement → BOTH
- Neither or only passive verbs (notified, received, employed by) → REJECTED

Step 4: Compare your independent assessment with the proposed type.
- Matches and no name corrections needed → CONFIRMED
- Matches but name was corrected (Rules 2-3) → CORRECTED
- Does not match but agency is valid → CORRECTED with new type
- Agency should be rejected → REJECTED

Step 5: Final recommendation.
- INCLUDE only if: passes all rules, evidence has clear linkage, type is confirmed or corrected
- EXCLUDE if: federal agency, missing geography, no clear linkage, weak/passive evidence
</verification_steps>

<missed_agency_recovery>
IMPORTANT: After verifying the agency above, perform a RECALL RECOVERY check.

Review the source document and the full extraction context. Identify any agencies that:
1. Are mentioned in the source text with clear linkage to an action
2. Are NOT already present in the full extraction context
3. Fit one of the agency types in <common_agency_types>

Pay special attention to these commonly missed patterns:
- DUAL ROLES: An agency already extracted as RESPONDING that also investigated the use of force
  (or vice versa). Check if the agency appears in only one role but should appear in both.
- DISTRICT ATTORNEYS: DA offices that investigated or reviewed the officer-involved shooting,
  declined to prosecute, or presented findings. These are often mentioned briefly.
- MEDICAL EXAMINERS / CORONERS: Mentioned in connection with autopsy, cause of death, or
  manner of death determination. These are INVESTIGATING agencies.
- STATE-LEVEL AGENCIES: California DOJ, state corrections, Arizona DPS, etc. These are sometimes
  mentioned as conducting independent investigations.
- OVERSIGHT BODIES: OIG, Police Accountability departments, Police Commissions — often mentioned
  in procedural language about review processes.
- SECONDARY RESPONDING AGENCIES: Additional police departments, sheriffs, or highway patrol
  units that assisted at the scene but are not the primary agency.

For each missed agency found, provide:
- agency_name (normalized, with geographic identifier)
- agency_type (RESPONDING or INVESTIGATING)
- evidence (passage from source text with clear linkage)
- role_description (brief description of their role)

If no missed agencies are found, return an empty list.
</missed_agency_recovery>
