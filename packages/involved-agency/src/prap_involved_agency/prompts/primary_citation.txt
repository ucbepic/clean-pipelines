<definitions>
{% if agency_type == "INVESTIGATING" %}
INVESTIGATING AGENCY = an agency that investigates the USE OF FORCE or OFFICER MISCONDUCT, NOT the underlying crime.

This is about inter-law-enforcement accountability. We are looking for agencies that reviewed, investigated, or made decisions about WHETHER AN OFFICER'S ACTIONS WERE JUSTIFIED — not agencies that investigated the burglary, robbery, homicide, or other criminal incident that led to the use of force.

VALID investigating citations (investigating the USE OF FORCE or MISCONDUCT):
- Agency investigated the officer-involved shooting
- Agency reviewed whether the use of force was justified
- Agency determined the shooting was lawful
- Agency declined to file charges against the officer
- Agency made a charging decision regarding the officer's conduct
- Coroner determined cause of death (inherently about the force outcome)
- Internal Affairs investigated the officer's use of force
- Agency investigated the deputy-involved incident
- Agency reviewed the officers conduct and found it within policy
- Oversight board reviewed the use of force

INVALID investigating citations (investigating the UNDERLYING CRIME, not the force):
- Agency investigated the burglary
- Agency investigated the robbery
- Detectives interviewed witnesses to the crime
- Agency responded to and investigated a call for service
- Agency investigated the homicide of the victim (when victim is NOT the person force was used on)
- Agency collected evidence at the crime scene (routine police work)
- Agency arrested the suspect

CRITICAL DISTINCTION: If an agency investigated a burglary and during that burglary an officer shot someone, the agency that investigated the burglary is a RESPONDING agency (their officers were involved in the incident). The agency that then investigated WHETHER THE SHOOTING WAS JUSTIFIED is the INVESTIGATING agency.
{% else %}
RESPONDING AGENCY = an agency whose officers participated in or were present at the incident.

VALID responding citations:
- Officer Name of Agency responded to scene
- Agency officers were present at the scene
- Officer Name of Agency used force (fired weapon, taser, pepper spray, baton, K9, control hold)
- Agency initiated or joined pursuit
- Agency officers secured the scene
- Agency officers collected evidence at scene
- Agency officers transported suspect
- For misconduct: Officer Name of Agency violated policy, witnessed misconduct, reported misconduct

INVALID responding citations:
- Officer employed by Agency (without incident involvement)
- Agency was notified (passive administrative)
- Agency mentioned without incident context
{% endif %}
</definitions>
