Your task is to review documents about a police incident to determine if it involved: (1) use of force, (2) sustained misconduct findings, and (3) officer-involved shooting with metal bullets.

CRITICALLY IMPORTANT: You are looking for a SINGLE, SPECIFIC incident - not a collection or summary of multiple incidents. If the document describes multiple incidents or appears to be a compilation (like an annual report or multiple case summaries), you MUST classify use of force as "No" or "Unclear" to signal potential file corruption.

Guidelines for classification:

1. USE OF FORCE includes but is not limited to
   - Physical control holds or takedowns
   - Strikes with hands, feet, or objects
   - Impact weapons (batons, flashlights)
   - Chemical agents (pepper spray, tear gas)
   - Electronic control devices (Tasers)
   - Pointing or discharge of firearms
   - K9 deployments with contact
   - Vehicle interventions (PIT maneuvers)
   - Enhanced restraints beyond standard handcuffing

   NOT use of force:
   - Verbal commands/threats
   - Officer presence only
   - Autopsy reports describing wounds without explicitly stating officer actions
   - Medical examiner findings without direct description of officer actions
   - Descriptions of injuries without explicit mention of what officers did
   - Lists of multiple incidents from different dates/cases
   - Collections of incidents in a report format

2. SUSTAINED MISCONDUCT means:
   - Explicit sustained/upheld findings
   - Confirmed disciplinary actions
   - Final, not preliminary determinations
   - Only if there was an investigation that had sustained findings of misconduct can there be misconduct
   NOT misconduct:
   - Mere allegations, no matter how much they might seem to imply misconduct
   - Pending investigations do not matter unless they result in sustained misconduct
   - Inferred misconduct without sustained findings
   - Other sustained types, such as sustained injuries

3. OFFICER-INVOLVED SHOOTING (OIS) requires:
   - Discharge of firearm with metal bullets BY AN OFFICER
   - Clear statement that officer(s) shot at a person
   NOT OIS:
   - Less-lethal weapons (tasers, bean bags, rubber bullets)
   - Brandishing without discharge
   - Threats only
   - Autopsy reports listing gunshot wounds without explicitly stating they came from police officers
   - Medical examiner descriptions of injuries without stating they were caused by police firearms
